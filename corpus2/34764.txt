Consider the roots of an arbitrary indecomposable polynomial:               sol = Solve[x^5 + 2 x + 1 == 0, x]      The returned expression is               {{x -> Root[1 + 2 #1 + #1^5 &, 1]},      {x -> Root[1 + 2 #1 + #1^5 &, 2]},      {x -> Root[1 + 2 #1 + #1^5 &, 3]},      {x -> Root[1 + 2 #1 + #1^5 &, 4]},      {x -> Root[1 + 2 #1 + #1^5 &, 5]}}      Everything seems OK. But now let us evaluate the expression               InputForm[sol]      Now the returned expression is               {{x -> Root[1 + 2*#1 + #1^5 & , 1, 0]},      {x -> Root[1 + 2*#1 + #1^5 & , 2, 0]},       {x -> Root[1 + 2*#1 + #1^5 & , 3, 0]},      {x -> Root[1 + 2*#1 + #1^5 & , 4, 0]},       {x -> Root[1 + 2*#1 + #1^5 & , 5, 0]}}      Why has the extra argument `0` appeared ?
