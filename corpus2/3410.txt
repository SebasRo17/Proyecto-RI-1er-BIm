The post office metric space, $P$ has the distance function defined as follows: $$ d_P (\mathbf{x},\mathbf{y}) := \begin{cases} 0 & \mathbf{x} = \mathbf{y}\\ \Vert \mathbf{x}\Vert_2+\Vert \mathbf{y}\Vert_2 & \mathbf{x}\neq \mathbf{y} \end{cases} $$ where $\Vert \mathbf{x}\Vert_2 = \sqrt{x_1^2+x_2^2}$ is the Euclidean distance from $\mathbf{x}=(x1,x2) \in \mathbb{R}^2$ to the origin. I am interested in drawing the balls of this metric centered at point $\mathbf{p}$, having a radius $r$: $$B_r(\mathbf{p}) \triangleq \{\mathbf{x} \in P\vert\ d(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{p})<r \}$$ Does anyone have any tips on how to do this?
